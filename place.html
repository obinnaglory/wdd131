About Owerri

Owerri, the capital of Imo State in southeastern Nigeria, is often called the “Heartland of the East” because of its central location among the Igbo states. It is a fast-growing city and a hub for commerce, culture, education, and entertainment. The people of Owerri are predominantly Igbo, known for their hospitality, vibrant traditions, festivals, music, and masquerades. The city is home to key institutions like FUTO, IMSU, and Alvan Ikoku College of Education. Economically, Owerri thrives on hospitality, nightlife, trade, and education, earning it the nickname “Las Vegas of Nigeria.” Agriculture also contributes through crops like palm produce, cassava, and yam. With a tropical climate, temperatures range between 20°C and 30°C. Attractions include Mbari Cultural Centre, Oguta Lake, and the Imo State Zoological Garden.

Top Attractions

  • Mbari Cultural and Art Centre – a showcase of Igbo art and tradition.
  • Oguta Lake – a popular tourist spot.
  • Imo State Zoological Garden.
  • Hotels, event centers, and shopping complexes.
