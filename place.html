About Owerri

Owerri, the capital of Imo State in southeastern Nigeria, is often called the “Heartland of the East” because of its central location among the Igbo states. It is a fast-growing city and a hub for commerce, culture, education, and entertainment. The people of Owerri are predominantly Igbo, known for their hospitality, vibrant traditions, festivals, music, and masquerades. The city is home to key institutions like the Federal University of Technology Owerri (FUTO), Imo State University (IMSU), and Alvan Ikoku Federal College of Education, making it an educational center in the region. Economically, Owerri thrives on hospitality, nightlife, trade, and education, earning it the nickname “Las Vegas of Nigeria” due to its many hotels, clubs, and relaxation spots, while agriculture also contributes through crops like palm produce, cassava, and yam. With a tropical climate of rainy (April–October) and dry (November–March) seasons, temperatures range between 20°C and 30°C. Notable attractions include the Mbari Cultural and Art Centre, Oguta Lake, and the Imo State Zoological Garden. Altogether, Owerri is a lively, modern city with deep cultural roots and growing economic significance in southeastern Nigeria.

Top Attractions

  • Mbari Cultural and Art Centre – a showcase of Igbo art, culture, and tradition.
  • Oguta Lake (nearby) – a popular tourist spot.
  • Imo State Zoological Garden.
  • Numerous hotels, event centers, and modern shopping complexes.
